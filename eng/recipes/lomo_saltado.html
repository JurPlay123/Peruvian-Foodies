Lomo saltado

Peru's gastronomy is one of the most praised in the world. the world. Although, there are several typical dishes that stand out, one of the most requested and delicious is the lomo saltado, a fusion of Peruvian saltado, which is a fusion of Peruvian and Chinese seasoning and Chinese, which results in a particular flavour. - https://www.larepublica.pe

Ingredients:

4

  • 3/4 of a kilo of thin loin
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 1 yellow chilli
  • 1/2 to 1 cup of sillao
  • 2 tablespoons red vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon ground garlic
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Chopped coriander
  • Vegetable oil, quantity required
  • 4 portions of cooked white rice

Preparation:

8 min

  1. Cut the loin into thick strips. Season with a pinch of salt, pepper and crushed garlic.

  2. Next, cut the tomatoes and onions into thick wedges. Also, cut the yellow chilli into thin strips.

  3. Then cut the white potatoes into sticks. Fry them in hot oil.

  4. Place a large frying pan on the heat. Heat it and when it is hot, add a dash of vegetable oil and when it starts to smoke, immediately add the loin and fry it without moving it. When the meat is browned on one side, start to sauté and flambé. If you wish, add a splash of pisco after the meat to flambé (do this carefully, because the heat can be high).

  5. Add the onions, yellow chilli and vinegar. Keep sautéing.

  6. Add the tomatoes and the sillao. Keep sautéing.

  7. Add the coriander, stir and serve hot with the chips and white rice.

Tips:

  • You can mix the potatoes with the sautéed potatoes so that they can soak up the juices, or leave them on the side so that they don't lose their crunchiness.
