Turrón de
Doña Pepa

The Turrón de Doña Pepais one of the best Peruvian dishes.

Ingredients (8 to 10 servings)

  • 35 grams white sesame seeds
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoons aniseed grains
  • 180 ml water
  • 3 drops of egg yellow dye
  • 338 grams of vegetable shortening
  • 750 grams of plain flour
  • 15 grams of salt
  • 4 grams of bicarbonate of soda
  • 8 egg yolks
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons vanilla essence

Honey:

  • 2 water apples in chunks, with peel
  • 1/2 pineapple, chopped
  • 1/2 pineapple peel
  • 2 quinces, in pieces, with peel
  • 1 orange
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 whole cinnamon stick
  • 5 cloves
  • 1 fig leaf
  • 1 and 1/2 caps of chancaca
  • 500 grams of white sugar
  • 60 grams of glucose
  • Water, quantity needed

For decoration:

  • 10 grams of pepelmas or coloured tablets
  • 10 grams of candies
  • 25 grams of dragees

Preparation (3 to 4 hours)


  1. Roast the chilli and aniseed. Leave to cool, and grind in a mortar or pestle. Set aside.

  2. Mix 180 ml of water with the yellow egg colouring. Set aside.

  3. Cream the vegetable shortening. Set aside.

  4. In a bowl, sift the flour together with the baking soda and baking powder. Add the reserved sesame seeds and aniseed.

  5. Make a well in the centre of the mixture, like a volcano, and place the creamed butter, the egg yolks, the reserved colouring water and the vanilla in it.

  6. Start to melt the butter with your fingers and then press the dough together with your hands until you have a homogeneous dough. Wrap in cling film and refrigerate for one hour.

  7. After this time, roll out the dough on a floured surface until it is one centimetre thick. Cut into sticks with a ruler, and bake in a floured baking tin. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes in a preheated oven at 180 °C.

Honey:


  1. Place the fruits in a pot. Cover with water and add the cinnamon, cloves and fig leaf. Let it boil for 30 minutes. When the fruit is cooked, strain it.

  2. Place the chancaca in a pot and pour 1/2 cup of water. Let the chancaca melt and strain to remove impurities.

  3. Place the fruit water in a saucepan. Add the melted chancaca, glucose and sugar. Stir until it starts to boil. Let it come to a soft ball. Turn off the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.

Assembly:


  1. Staple a 22 x 12 cm rectangular baking tin, and place the baked sticks side by side. If any gaps remain, cover them with scraped sticks. Press to seal.

  2. Place a little honey on the sticks and let it drip off on its own. Do not pour too much honey or move it around too much because it will crystallise.

  3. Put the second layer of biscuits, but in the opposite direction to the first layer of dough. Then add honey.

  4. Continue until you have completed 3 rows of dough. Finish with honey.

  5. Decorate with pepelmas, confectionery and dragées.

  6. Leave to stand for an hour before unmoulding.
